About

I’m a software engineer living in San Francisco. I grew up in good old New Hampshire and went to school there too. At Dartmouth College, I earned a bachelor’s degree in Physics with a minor in Engineering.

My introduction to computer science was actually through a physics class. I learned my first algorithm, Shor’s algorithm for integer factorization, in a quantum computing class. I then realized that the field of computer science has an expansive theoretical foundation and to this day is full of interesting, unanswered questions. Once I familiarized myself with some of the mathematical underpinnings of computer science, I decided to gain practical software engineering experience. After finishing college, I went on to complete the coding bootcamp, App Academy.

Shortly thereafter, I joined Lending Club as an associate software engineer. I started working on the front end. I needed to pick up Javascript, Node, and internal frameworks along the way, which has been a lot of fun since I love to learn. Trying to get closer to my mathematical roots, I’ve been eagerly moving down the stack and have started learning Java in order to work on Lending Club’s back-end microservice architecture.

I’ve been really happy with my decision to pursue software engineering because computers are today’s most powerful tool for solving problems. As I continue my career in software engineering, I look forward to tackling more complex and important problems.
