About

I’m a web developer living in San Francisco.

I grew up in good old New Hampshire and went to school there too. At Dartmouth College, I got a BA Physics with a minor in Engineering.

The coding bug bit me one day when I had to create a graph to show the autocorrelation of a 2 dimensional annular ring for a physics class on Optics. The calculations were really annoying and I would have had to do many calculations for different sized rings. Instead of spending my time with pen and paper, I sat down at my keyboard a banged out a Matlab script to generate the plot for me. It may have taken a little bit longer, but the satisfaction at the end was worth it.

Since then, I knew coding was where it’s at. I started learning Ruby on my own. I applied and was accepted to App Academy, an intensive, 12 week long web development bootcamp. I learned the full stack there. Ruby, Rails, JavaScript, jQuery, Backbone.js, HTML, CSS, SQL, git.

It feels great to have these tools under my belt. Check out some of the things I’ve made!
